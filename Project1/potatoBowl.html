Intro

I make this for breakfast all the time. It's quick, filling, and you can prep the ingredients whenever you want. I often prep extra so that the next day I can just throw it all together the next day. This is just a base, so you can throw whatever extras you want. Cheese, sauces, mushrooms, ham, all kinds of extra goodies would go perfectly in this.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs: $0.40
  • 1 medium Russet potatoe: $0.40
  • 1 small jalapeno: $0.10
  • 1/2 bell pepper: $0.50
  • 1/2 onion: $0.20
  • 1 Tbsp cooking oil

Directions

  1. Small dice your produce.
  2. Put a medium pan on medium-high heat, add a tablespoon of cooking oil.
  3. Add the potatoes and toss so that they are covered in the oil. Cook for ~five minutes, tossing occasionally.
  4. Crack your eggs into a small non-stick pan. Cook however you prefer, but I prefer sunny-side up for reasons I'll make clear in the tips.
  5. Add the peppers and onions to the potatoes, give it a quick toss, and cook until tender, ~2 minutes.
  6. Add salt and pepper, then toss before putting it into a bowl. Slide your eggs on top and you're ready to enjoy!

Tips

So you can use whatever cooking oil you want, but I recommend some cheap olive oil. The expensive extra virgin stuff has a very low smoke point. You want to do a dice that's about 1 cm thick. You can dice it larger but it will take longer to cook. I like sunny-side or over easy eggs with this recipe. When you crack the yolk, the heat from the cooked produce will cook it a little bit so that it becomes like a nice thick sauce.
