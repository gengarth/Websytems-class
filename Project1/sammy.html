Intro

This is a bit on the higher side, and you may be skeptical of some of the ingredient prices. But its just a great example of the use leftovers and taking advantage of deals. Everything except the steak and onions were bought on a BOGO, which essentially takes the unit price down by half. The steak was a great deal I got on some sirloin, $6/lb. I grilled it and ate half, now we get to repurpose it into a sandwich. You could use any cut but the cheapest cuts you'll want to tenderize a bit. Caloriewise, this thing is a meal in itself, but if you want a bit more balanced nutrition, it goes great with some fries or a basic salad.

Ingredients

Sauce

  • 1 Tbsp mayo: $0.05
  • 1 Tbsp dijon mustard: $0.10
  • 1 Tbsp horseradish: $0.11

Sandwich

  • 8oz cooked steak: $3
  • 1 bun $0.33
  • 1/4 onion: $0.10
  • Butter

Directions

  1. Heat a medium pan on medium heat.
  2. Medium dice your onion and slice your steak into thin strips.
  3. Butter the faces of your buns and place butter side down on the skillet ~2 minutes then remove
  4. Add your steak. Flip after ~2 minutes
  5. Add your onions and season with salt and pepper. Let that all fry together for ~2 minutes
  6. Whip together equal parts mayo, mustard, and horseradish.
  7. Build your sandwich with the sauce first, then onions, then the steak, then a bit more sauce on the top bun. And you're all done!

Tips

When you slice the steak do it agaisnt the grain so it's more tender. You may think toasting the bun is optional. It's not. Even a toasted bun isi going to get a bit soggy with all that steak juice, much less an untoasted one. Same for the build order. You want those onions on the bottom to provide as much of a barrier as possible. The steak is probably already seasoned from the orginial cook, but you want to season it in the pan again anyway. When you slice it up, you essentially create a lot more unseasoned surface area that needs to be corrected.
